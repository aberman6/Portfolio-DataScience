About Me

About Me

I'm a socially-minded data scientist with a fascination for human behavior and decision-making. From public health, to energy conservation, to business metrics, to human cognition, you name it, I'm into analyzing it.

Past

I've walked a winding career path. Starting in brain and cognition research, to evaluating nonprofit efficacy, to consulting clients around technology data, to a Masters in Data Science at Duke. I love where I've found myself; at the intersection of computation, statistics, psychology, and public policy.

Present

I bring data to life through engaging storytelling. Technical results mean nothing if they can't be communicated to non-technical stakeholders. It's my job to find meaning in your data and to make sure that meaning is clearly understood.

Detecting Solar PV Through Supervised Learning

As the solar industry grows in size, it’s increasingly important to monitor consumer solar adoption. The first step in understanding consumer energy consumption is to delineate homes with and without solar panels. To address this first step, myself and my colleagues evaluated several modeling approaches to identifying solar PV in high resolution aerial imagery data. A convolutional neural network (CNN) approach yielded high rates of accuracy of solar PV detection from satellite aerial imagery.
